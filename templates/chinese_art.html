Chinese Arts Analysis


The Chinese emperors of all dynasties collected many cultural relics. The royal collection of the Qing Dynasty(1636–1912) has reached an unprecedented scale. These collections are stored in the emperor's palaces and royal gardens. The most famous one is Yuanmingyuan, aka Summer Palace. In 1860, the British and French allied forces robbed and burned the Old Summer Palace.

For my analysis, this data has a limitation. It provided the culture, accession year, and credit line, but it didn't state where the items originated. But as you can see from the accession year, all the spikes pretty much aligns with the historical events.

I then take a deep dive into the credit line and found interesting facts about Heber Bishop, who donated all his collections to MET in 1902.

Bishop is a businessman. He began to collect artworks (particularly jade) from all over the world in the 1870s, first began to buy some exquisite Chinese Jades in New York and Boston. Later, he learned that these jades came from the European continent and were plundered from the Chinese palace and royal garden by the British and French allied forces during the Second Opium War in 1860. Following this clue, Bishop went to England and the European continent several times; he went to London, Frankfurt, Amsterdam, Dresden, Berlin, Vienna, St. Petersburg, and Moscow, where he obtained many of the most exquisite jade collections. He also wrote the book Investigations and study in jade. Before his death in 1902, he donated more than 1000 pieces of jade to the MET, which is the first and most important collection of jade in the American Museum.
